Mortgage Rates Have Fallen 120bps From Peak But Remain Twice the Pre-2022 Average

30-Year Fixed Mortgage Rate (MORTGAGE30US) · 2019–2024
Key Evidence 30Y mortgage rate peaked at 7.8% in Oct 2023 — highest since 2000 — and has since fallen to ~6.6%. Yet pre-2022 borrowers locked in rates near 3–4%, creating a "lock-in effect" suppressing existing home sales to 30-year lows.
  • 9 FRED series tracked from 2000–present
  • 30Y, 15Y, and 5/1 ARM rates all remain historically elevated
  • Lock-in effect: ~85% of mortgages outstanding carry sub-5% rates
  • Existing home sales fell to 3.84M — lowest since Oct 2010
Diagnostic Analytics

FHA Delinquency at 11.5% Signals Concentrated Stress in Lower-Income Borrowers, Not Broad Credit Deterioration

Delinquency Rate Comparison — Q4 2025
Key Evidence FHA delinquency (11.52%) is 2.7× the overall rate (4.26%) and rising — reaching its highest level since Q2 2021. The divergence is not random: FHA borrowers are lower-income, first-time buyers whose delinquency tracks labor market stress, not just interest rates.
  • Cause: expired pandemic-era FHA forbearance + labor market disparities
  • Overall rate rose only +28bps YoY — headline masks concentration of stress
  • Geographic cluster: Texas Sun Belt metros (+1.3pp), Maryland, Nevada
  • Foreclosure inventory still near historic lows — not a systemic signal yet
Actionable Recommendation

Use FHA Delinquency as an Early Warning Signal Before Rotating Into Non-QM Subordinated Tranches

📌 Action: Monitor FHA delinquency trajectory quarterly alongside the FRED Delinquency tab before adding exposure to subordinated Non-QM RMBS tranches from 2022–2024 vintages.
↓
✅ Expected Outcome: Avoid buying into sub-tranche spread compression while credit stress is still rising in the FHA segment — which historically leads Non-QM vintage stress by 1–2 quarters.
Evidence: KBRA decade study shows 3.8% CDR → 0.03% realized loss when underwriting is sound, but non-prime profiles reach 8–10% CDR. FHA stress is an upstream signal for collateral pool risk in similarly-rated borrower segments.
Why This Matters for Apollo ABF Apollo has deployed $44B+ across residential mortgage and Non-QM RMBS over a decade. The dashboard's Delinquency tab directly surfaces the DRSFRMACBS series used to time entry/exit decisions in subordinated tranches. Monitoring FHA as a leading indicator is standard practice for credit traders in this asset class.
