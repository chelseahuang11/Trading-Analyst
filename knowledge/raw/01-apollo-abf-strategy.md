# Apollo's Asset-Backed Finance Strategy

## Overview

Apollo participates in asset-backed finance (ABF) investments through diverse channels, sourcing opportunities via proprietary platforms and partnerships while engaging in both primary and secondary markets.

## Investment Approach

Apollo's ABF strategy encompasses:

- **Diversified asset types** including commercial real estate, residential mortgages, financial assets (inventory, receivables, structured debt), hard assets (transportation, infrastructure), and consumer finance
- **Dual market participation** in broadly syndicated asset-backed securitization markets and private direct origination
- **Risk mitigation** through structuring, collateral assessment, and contractual protections

The firm describes its approach as enabling "risk-adjusted return, accounting for structure, asset risk and yield."

## Key Advantages

Apollo highlights these competitive attributes:

- "Price Certainty," "Lender Alignment," and "Relationship Simplicity" for private ABF
- Broad global relationships with proprietary origination capabilities
- Long-term investor alignment with scaled, diverse, and stable capital
- Active participation across private and traditional markets

## Solutions Portfolio

- Securitized Products
- Warehouse Financing
- Platform Structured Equity
- Whole Loans & Assets
- Portfolio Finance
- Private and bilateral ABL facilities

## Notable Transaction

Apollo structured the largest-ever music rights asset-backed securitization, a $1.8 billion transaction with Concord in December 2022.

## Investment Strategies

Apollo's ABF-related strategies include Asset-Backed, Multi-Asset Credit, and Opportunistic approaches targeting downside protection and alpha generation.

## RMBS Track Record

For over a decade, Apollo and its affiliates have deployed over $44 billion across a variety of residential mortgage loans, non-agency residential mortgage-backed securities (RMBS), agency RMBS, mortgage servicing rights financing and home equity financing opportunities while experiencing minimal losses.

## 2024 Performance Highlights

- Management fees showed 22% year-over-year growth, with 25% growth in credit
- Strong origination volumes and attractive spreads in asset-backed and high-grade businesses
- More than $200 billion annualized origination capacity across corporate, asset-based and structured finance
- Apollo's Credit business is its largest asset management strategy by AUM
