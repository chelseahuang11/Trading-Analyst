# U.S. Mortgage Delinquency Rate Finishes 2025 Flat Year-Over-Year — Cotality

## National Overview

**Overall Delinquency Rate:** The national mortgage delinquency rate remained at **3.2%** in December 2025, unchanged from December 2024.

**Key Insight:** According to Molly Boesel, Senior Principal Economist at Cotality, "Delinquency activity ended 2025 on a remarkably even footing" with "very little directional movement over the past several months."

## Delinquency Breakdown by Stage

- **Early-Stage Delinquencies (30–59 days past due):** 1.6% — unchanged year-over-year
- **Adverse Delinquency (60–89 days past due):** 0.5% — unchanged year-over-year
- **Serious Delinquency (90+ days past due, including foreclosures):** 1.1% — up from 1.0% in December 2024
- **Transition Rate (current to 30 days past due):** 0.7% — down from 0.8% in December 2024

## Foreclosure Status

Foreclosure inventory remained near historic lows, continuing its trend of hovering close to record-low levels.

## Geographic Variations

**States with Highest Increases:**
- Maryland: +0.4 percentage points
- Nevada: +0.3 percentage points

**Metropolitan Areas with Largest Delinquency Increases:**
- Odessa, Texas: +1.3 percentage points
- Laredo, Texas: +0.9 percentage points
- Danville, Illinois; Lima, Ohio; Jonesboro, Arkansas; McAllen-Edinburg-Mission, Texas: +0.6 percentage points each

**Metro Areas with Rising Delinquencies:** Approximately 184 of 384 U.S. metropolitan areas experienced year-over-year increases in overall delinquency rates, with 183 areas seeing serious delinquency rate increases.

## Historical Context

The current 3.2% rate remains significantly below the 12% peak recorded in 2010, indicating that despite pockets of stress, the overall mortgage market remains in sound condition by long-term historical standards.
