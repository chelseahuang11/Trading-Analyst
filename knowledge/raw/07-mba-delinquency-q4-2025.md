# Mortgage Delinquencies Increase in the Fourth Quarter of 2025 — MBA

## Overview

The Mortgage Bankers Association (MBA) National Delinquency Survey reported an increase in mortgage delinquency rates in Q4 2025.

## Key Statistics

The delinquency rate for mortgage loans on one-to-four-unit residential properties increased to **4.26%** at the end of the fourth quarter of 2025:
- Up 27 basis points from the third quarter of 2025
- Up 28 basis points from one year ago
- Despite the modest increase from mid-2024 lows, the December 2025 delinquency rate remains well below the 12% peak recorded in 2010

## FHA Loan Performance

FHA loans reached a delinquency rate of **11.52%**, the highest level since the second quarter of 2021.

**Contributing factors:**
- Expiration of pandemic-era FHA relief options
- Disparities in the labor market — a key determinant of mortgage delinquency levels

## Geographic Breadth of Stress

Nearly half of metropolitan areas experienced an uptick in:
- Overall delinquencies
- Serious delinquencies
- Foreclosures

This breadth of localized increases suggests that pockets of households are coming under greater financial strain, even if it's not yet visible in the national aggregates.

## Long-Term Performance Context

On average, about **1.3%** of mortgage balances became seriously delinquent during 2025 — a share that looks very similar to the averages observed outside of the period around the Great Recession (when delinquencies exceeded 8%).

## Market Implications for RMBS

Rising FHA delinquencies at the subordinate tranche level may pressure non-agency RMBS deals containing government-insured collateral, while prime jumbo and non-QM deals with stronger borrower profiles continue to exhibit resilient performance.
