# Where Are Mortgage Delinquencies Rising the Most? — Federal Reserve Bank of New York

## Overview

The Federal Reserve Bank of New York's Center for Microeconomic Data released its Quarterly Report on Household Debt and Credit for Q4 2025.

## Aggregate Household Debt (Q4 2025)
- Total household debt reached **$18.8 trillion**, up $191 billion
- Mortgage balances grew to **$13.2 trillion** (+$98 billion)
- Credit card debt increased to **$1.28 trillion** (+$44 billion)

## Mortgage Delinquency Trends

**Current Status:** "The delinquency rate for mortgages — although still near low levels on a longer-term basis — has been steadily increasing over the past few years."

About **1.3%** of mortgage balances became seriously delinquent during 2025, comparable to pre-Great Recession averages (outside crisis periods when delinquencies exceeded 8%).

## Geographic and Income-Based Breakdown

### By Income Level

The analysis reveals stark disparities based on borrower income:

- **Lowest-income zip codes:** Delinquency rates surged from ~0.5% in 2021 to nearly 3.0% by late 2025
- **Highest-income areas:** Maintained historically lower delinquency rates, appearing "largely insulated from these pressures"
- **Middle-income quartiles:** Showed intermediate trends with moderate increases

### Regional Labor Market Correlation

A strong relationship exists between unemployment changes and delinquency rates:

- Counties with rising unemployment of 1.6+ percentage points experienced delinquency increases of ~0.6 percentage points
- Counties with stable/declining unemployment saw more modest increases (~0.2 percentage points)

### Housing Market Impact

Home price changes correlate with delinquency patterns, though less strongly than labor market factors:
- Areas with falling home prices experienced rising delinquency rates
- Gulf Coast Florida saw pronounced home price declines
- National home prices rose 1.0% year-over-year as of November 2025

## Key Contributing Factors

**Labor Market Weakness:** "Counties experiencing the steepest increases in unemployment saw a notable worsening in mortgage delinquency."

**Credit Quality:** The median credit score for newly originated mortgages has remained above 750 since 2009, reflecting tight lending standards.

## Context and Caveats

Overall mortgage performance "continues to perform well by historical standards" despite recent increases. The deterioration follows artificially low pandemic-era levels supported by stimulus and forbearances. Authors emphasize that while aggregate figures appear stable, "lower-income areas and in areas experiencing worsening labor market or housing market conditions" show concerning acceleration trends.

## RMBS Implications

The income and geographic stratification of delinquencies has direct implications for RMBS collateral pool analysis — deals with heavy concentrations in lower-income geographies or labor-market-stressed metros warrant closer scrutiny of credit enhancement levels.
