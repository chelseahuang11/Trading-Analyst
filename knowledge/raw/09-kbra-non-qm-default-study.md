# KBRA Non-QM Default Study: A Decade of Insights

## Study Overview

KBRA analyzed over **475,000 loans** representing **$216.7 billion** in original balance from nearly **600 NQM transactions** issued between 2015 and April 2025. This comprehensive research examines performance across 15+ loan characteristics.

## Default Rates & Credit Losses

- Weighted average cumulative default rate: **3.8%**
- Realized credit losses remain minimal, averaging just **0.03%**
- Of defaulted loans studied, approximately 6,606 experienced losses through forbearance or capitalized amounts
- Involuntary liquidations: just over 300 loans faced meaningful losses with an average severity of **26.5%**

## Credit Profile Impact

Borrowers with prior credit events demonstrate significantly elevated default risk. Non-prime profiles "have shown meaningfully higher default rates than those without, ranging between approximately **8% and 10%**."

## Vintage Analysis

Deal performance varies considerably by issuance year:

| Vintage | Cumulative Default Rate (excl. COVID) |
|---------|---------------------------------------|
| 2019    | ~5.5%                                 |
| 2020    | ~5.0%                                 |
| 2022    | ~4.0%                                 |
| 2023    | ~4.1%                                 |

The COVID-19 pandemic substantially impacted 2019–2020 loan performance.

## Key Risk Factors

### Credit Scores
- FICO scores below 660: default rates near **10%**
- FICO scores above 760: default rates below **2%**

### Loan-to-Value (CLTV)
- CLTVs of 85% or higher: default rates of **5.5%**
- CLTVs of 65%–70%: default rates of **4.1%**
- Higher CLTV ratios correlate with default increases

### Documentation Type
- Alt Doc loans have defaulted at rates **12.9% higher** than Full Doc loans on average
- Full documentation loans significantly outperform alternatives

## Risk Management Observations

Lenders employ compensating factors for higher-risk attributes, narrowing performance variation. Owner-occupied and investor-occupied loans show comparable results partly due to "differences in required FICO scores and CLTV ratios between the two occupancy types."

## Market Significance

Since 2015, a new tier of the private-label residential mortgage-backed securities market has emerged, with securities collateralized by non-qualified mortgage loans. These securities enable mortgage lenders to serve borrowers with non-traditional credit profiles — including the self-employed, recent credit event borrowers, and real estate investors.
