# Non-Qualified Mortgage Securitization Market — RiskSpan

## Market Overview

Since 2015, a distinct segment of the private-label residential mortgage-backed securities (PLS) market has emerged focusing on non-qualified mortgage (non-QM) loans. This sector developed to serve borrowers with non-traditional credit profiles who were underserved following the 2008 financial crisis.

Post-crisis lending standards became significantly restrictive. The limited issuance of private-label RMBS since the financial crisis has generally consisted of new origination jumbo "prime" mortgage loans. The non-QM market emerged to address borrowers unable to meet the stringent "qualified mortgage" (QM) standards adopted by government-sponsored enterprises (GSEs).

## Non-QM Borrower Segments

The non-QM market serves multiple underserved populations:

- **Self-employed individuals** lacking traditional income documentation
- **Borrowers with credit blemishes** including recent foreclosures, short sales, or delinquencies
- **Investors** purchasing rental properties outside GSE parameters
- **Foreign nationals** without U.S. credit history
- **Borrowers seeking non-traditional products** featuring interest-only or balloon payment structures

Common non-QM product features:
- Bank statement programs (12 or 24-month reviews) for income verification
- Expanded debt-to-income (DTI) ratios beyond QM thresholds

## Market Structure & Growth

- Non-bank financial institutions have dominated non-QM originations
- Backed by asset managers, hedge funds, and private equity
- Securitization activity began in 2015 and has increased steadily since

## Credit Performance & Risk

Non-QM securities present elevated risks compared to jumbo prime offerings:

- Weaker borrower credit profiles
- Product features that deviate from QM standards
- Alternative income assessment methodologies
- "Non-QM securities are likely to experience more performance volatility and higher realized losses than their jumbo prime counterparts in negative economic scenarios"

Investors face challenges balancing increased loss risk against incremental yield premiums.

## Historical Context & Future Outlook

The article notes cyclical parallels with pre-crisis lending expansion. The current expansion of credit criteria raises questions about market memory regarding historical underwriting lessons and securitization performance. However, post-2015 non-QM issuance benefits from stronger structural protections, more rigorous rating agency oversight, and better data transparency than pre-crisis subprime RMBS.

## Implications for RMBS Investors

Non-QM RMBS investors must assess:
1. Collateral pool composition (documentation type, FICO distribution, LTV ratios)
2. Geographic concentration risk
3. Originator underwriting standards and track record
4. Structural credit enhancement levels relative to expected loss scenarios
