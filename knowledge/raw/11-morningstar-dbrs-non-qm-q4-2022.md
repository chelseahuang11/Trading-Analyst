# Morningstar DBRS: Q4 2022 Non-QM RMBS Performance Update

## Credit Performance Overview

The non-QM RMBS sector demonstrated resilience in Q4 2022. According to the report, "The credit performance of residential mortgage-backed securities (RMBS) backed by non-Qualified Mortgage (non-QM) loans rated by DBRS continued to remain steady in Q4 2022." Despite challenging market conditions, no significant deterioration emerged.

## Market Conditions and Headwinds

The sector faced substantial headwinds throughout Q4 2022:

- **Rising mortgage rates** that dampened borrower demand for home purchases and refinances
- **Falling home prices** in certain regions, though prices remained "relatively resilient" overall
- **Wider credit spreads**, particularly affecting subordinated bonds
- **Persistent economic uncertainty** regarding interest rate trajectories and recession timing

## Origination Activity

Loan origination continued but at notably reduced levels compared to pre-2022 volumes. For some lenders, origination "declined to a fraction of their peak origination volumes before 2022." Despite these challenges, funding for non-QM loans remained generally available, and lending activity continued.

## New Issuance Trends

Issuers maintained market presence despite challenging conditions. "Issuers continued to bring new deals to the market despite wider credit spreads," with subordinated bonds experiencing the greatest pricing pressure.

## Housing Market Outlook (from 2022)

The analysis projected that home prices would likely grow modestly long-term, potentially remaining stagnant or declining in areas that experienced recent rapid appreciation.

## Structural Considerations

- Senior tranches maintained stable performance driven by credit enhancement buffers
- Subordinated bonds bore the brunt of spread widening
- Deals with stronger borrower profiles (high FICO, lower LTV) outperformed weaker-collateral deals

## Lessons for RMBS Analysis

This period (2022) demonstrated that:
1. Non-QM RMBS can sustain performance through rate cycles when underwriting is sound
2. Spread widening in sub tranches creates potential entry points for credit investors
3. New issuance resilience signals the permanence of the non-QM market as a capital markets fixture
