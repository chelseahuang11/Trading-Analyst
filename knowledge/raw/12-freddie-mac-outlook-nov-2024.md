# Freddie Mac: Economic, Housing and Mortgage Market Outlook — November 2024

## Economic Overview

The U.S. economy demonstrated robust growth in Q3 2024:
- Real GDP expanded at a seasonally adjusted annual rate of **2.8%** (moderating from Q2's 3.0%)
- Consumer spending led expansion at **3.7%**, the fastest pace since Q1 2023, contributing ~2.5 percentage points to growth
- October payroll additions: only 12,000 jobs (disrupted by hurricanes and Boeing strike)
- Unemployment rate: steady at **4.1%**
- Wage growth: **4%** year-over-year
- Core PCE Price Index: rose **2.7%** from a year ago; goods prices declined 1.2% annually

## Housing and Mortgage Market Conditions

### Home Sales and Inventory
- Existing home sales fell to their lowest level since October 2010 at **3.84 million**
- New home sales: 738,000 in September, slightly above pre-pandemic averages
- Existing home inventory: **4.3 months' supply** in September — the highest since October 2020
- 5–6 months typically considered a balanced market

### Housing Construction
- Housing starts declined marginally: -0.7% from September 2023
- Multifamily construction contracted over **15%** from prior year
- Homebuilder confidence: Housing Market Index at 43, indicating poor near-term building conditions

### Home Prices and Affordability
- FHFA House Price Index: **4.2% annual growth** in August 2024
- Homeownership rate declined to **65.6%** in Q3 2024 (from 66% in Q3 2023)
- Renter-occupied units increased 1.1 million; owner-occupied units rose only 0.6 million

### Mortgage Rates
- 30-year fixed mortgage rates increased from September's 2-year low of **6.08%** to **6.72%** by late October
- October average: **6.43%**
- Rates rebounded rapidly beyond 6.7% by end of period

**Key Pattern:** Mortgage rates typically remain stable following Fed cuts, as bond markets price in anticipated reductions beforehand. Following six previous rate-cutting cycles, mortgage rates "increased on average 0.1 percentage points" six weeks after initial Fed cuts.

**Rate Lock-In Effect:** Intensified from $38,000 in September to $42,000 as of October 2024.

## 2025 Forecast

| Metric | Outlook |
|--------|---------|
| Economic Growth | Moderate deceleration from cooling labor market |
| Mortgage Rates | Gradual decline throughout 2025 |
| Home Sales | Modest increase driven by rate declines and improved inventory |
| Purchase Originations | Volume growth from higher sales and prices |
| Refinance Volume | Increase from rate declines |
| Total Originations | Modest increase |
| House Prices | Continued appreciation at slower pace |

## RMBS Implications

- Rate lock-in effect suppresses prepayment speeds, extending duration on existing MBS pools
- Declining mortgage rates in 2025 will gradually release locked-in supply, improving convexity profile of agency MBS
- Modest origination growth supports new non-agency issuance pipeline
