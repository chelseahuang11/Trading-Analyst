# Freddie Mac: Economic, Housing and Mortgage Market Outlook — September 2024

## Economic Conditions

The U.S. economy demonstrates continued expansion with moderating growth signals consistent with a soft landing scenario.
- Q2 2024 GDP: grew at an annualized rate of **3%** (up from 1.4% annualized in Q1 2024)
- Consumer spending: rising at 2.9% annualized in Q2 (up from 1.5% in Q1 2024)

**Labor Market Cooling:**
- August nonfarm payroll gains: 142,000 (below 12-month average of 202,000)
- Unemployment rate: ticked down to **4.2%** (slightly lower than July's highest rate since November 2021)
- Job openings: moderated from 7.9 million in June to **7.7 million** in July (lowest since January 2021)

**Inflation Moderating:**
- Core PCE Price Index: rose 0.2% month-over-month and **2.5%** year-over-year in July 2024
- Consumer Price Index: increased **2.9%** year-over-year (smallest 12-month increase since March 2021)

## Mortgage Rates and Market Activity

- 30-year fixed-rate mortgage: averaged **6.5%** in August; ended August at **6.35%**
- Despite falling rates, homebuyer activity remained subdued — prospective buyers "continue to wait for rates to fall further"
- Purchase applications: minimal movement despite four consecutive weeks of declining rates
- Refinance activity: responded more positively, accounting for **46.4%** of total application activity as of end of August

## Housing Market and Home Prices

- Total home sales (existing + new) for July: rose 2.6% month-over-month to **4.7 million**
- Pending home sales: all four U.S. regions declining in transactions monthly
- Homebuilder confidence: index deteriorated to **39** in August (below 50 threshold indicating poor building conditions)
- Housing starts: declined to a SAAR of **1.24 million**, 6.8% below June's 1.33 million units
- FHFA House Price Index: declined 0.1% month-over-month in June; rose **5.1% year-over-year**
- Vermont: leading appreciation at 13.4% annual appreciation

## Affordability Challenges

- Pending home sales index of **70.2** — the lowest reading since the index began tracking in 2001
- Affordability headwinds continue limiting homebuyer participation across the country

## Mortgage Origination Forecast (September 2024)

- Purchase mortgage origination volume to grow modestly
- Decline in mortgage rates to slightly boost refinance originations
- Total origination forecast: modest volume increase in 2024 and 2025

## Future Outlook

- Mortgage rates expected to decline further, though remaining above **6%** by year-end 2024
- Housing demand expected to improve, but home sales will "remain muted in 2024 and 2025" due to persisting affordability constraints
- Home prices: projected to continue appreciating at a modest pace in 2024 and 2025, supported by tight inventory
- Inventory constrained by rate lock effect — significant rate declines needed to encourage meaningful inventory increases

## RMBS Analytical Takeaways

- Sustained above-6% mortgage rates compress housing turnover, reducing prepayment speeds on seasoned RMBS pools
- Affordability constraints create persistent demand for non-QM/expanded credit products serving self-employed and non-traditional borrowers
- Tight inventory supports home price appreciation, which protects LTV ratios in existing RMBS collateral pools
