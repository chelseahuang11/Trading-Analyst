# Federal Reserve: Report on Economic Well-Being of U.S. Households in 2024 — Housing

## Homeownership Rates

**Overall Statistics:**
- Roughly **63%** of U.S. adults owned their homes in 2024
- **28%** rented

**Income Disparities:**
- Adults earning below $50,000: only **35%** owned homes
- Adults earning $100,000+: **85%** owned homes

**Demographic Disparities:**
- White households: **71%** ownership rate
- Asian households: **66%** ownership rate
- Hispanic households: **50%** ownership rate
- Black households: **47%** ownership rate
- Adults with disabilities: **52%** ownership (vs. 66% without disabilities)

**Age Considerations:**
- Younger adults under 60 earning $100,000+ were more than 3x as likely to own homes vs. peers earning under $50,000
- Older homeowners often owned properties outright, making income less determinative

## Mortgage Market Conditions

**Payment Amounts (2024):**
- Median monthly mortgage payment: **$1,500**
- Northeast: $1,600
- West: $1,811
- South: $1,500
- Midwest: $1,300

**Recent Purchasers (2023–2024):**
- Nationwide median for recent buyers: **$2,020/month**
- West: $3,220/month (highest in the nation)

## Rental Market Conditions

**Rent Costs:**
- Median monthly rent in 2024: **$1,200**
- YoY increases: ~10% annually since 2022 ($1,000 → $1,100 → $1,200)

**Regional Rent Levels:**
- West: $1,600
- Northeast: $1,300
- South: $1,080–$1,200
- Midwest: $900–$1,050

Recent movers paid ~$100–$200 more monthly than established renters.

## Renter Motivations and Challenges

**Primary Reasons for Renting (financial barriers dominated):**
- Over two-thirds couldn't afford down payments
- **49%** couldn't manage monthly mortgage payments
- **42%** failed mortgage qualification
- **58%** cited convenience; **39%** preferred renting to ownership

**Payment Difficulties:**
- 1 in 5 renters fell behind on payments during 2024 (unchanged from 2023)
- Lower-income renters (under $100,000): delinquency rate near **24%**
- Higher-income renters: **6%** delinquency rate

**Eviction Risk:**
- 2% of renters moved in the prior year because of eviction or threat of eviction (14% of all renters who relocated)

## Housing Affordability

**Neighborhood Satisfaction:**
- Only **38%** of adults felt satisfied with housing costs in their neighborhoods
- Renters: fewer than 30% approved of local housing expenses
- Homeowners: 43% satisfied

**Homeowners Insurance:**
- **7%** of all homeowners lacked insurance
- Ranges: 2% in New England to 13% in West-South Central
- Uninsured homeowners earning under $25,000: ~28–30%
- Of uninsured: **43%** cited affordability as primary obstacle

## Natural Disaster Financial Impact

- One-fifth of adults reported financial effects from natural disasters or extreme weather
- South Atlantic and West South Central: highest impact at **35%**
- Property damage affecting ~10% of the national population

## RMBS Analytical Implications

- Persistent affordability constraints create durable demand for non-agency RMBS collateral programs (non-QM, expanded credit)
- Rising rental costs compress household balance sheets, potentially pressuring delinquencies in lower-credit collateral pools
- Geographic concentration in disaster-prone regions (Gulf Coast, South) warrants haircuts in stressed scenarios for property valuation models
