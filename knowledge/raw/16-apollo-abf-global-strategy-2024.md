# Apollo Global Management: Credit Strategy & Asset-Backed Finance — 2024

## Overview

Apollo's Credit business is its largest asset management strategy by AUM, spanning the private and public corporate credit and asset-backed finance markets.

## 2024 Strategic Highlights

**Management Fee Growth:**
- 22% year-over-year growth in total management fees
- 25% growth in credit management fees, attributed to strong origination volumes and attractive spreads in asset-backed and high-grade businesses

**Origination Scale:**
- More than **$200 billion annualized origination capacity** across corporate, asset-based, and structured finance

**RMBS Track Record:**
- For over a decade, Apollo and its affiliates have deployed over **$44 billion** across:
  - Residential mortgage loans
  - Non-agency RMBS
  - Agency RMBS
  - Mortgage servicing rights (MSR) financing
  - Home equity financing opportunities
- Minimal losses experienced across this deployment history

## Global Expansion (2024)

- Deepening UK/EU middle-market and asset-backed finance presence
- Building distribution in Japan and Korea
- Capturing bank retrenchment opportunities (Basel III/IV capital requirements forcing banks to exit structured products)
- Meeting rising demand for private credit in Europe and Asia-Pacific

## ABF Investment Philosophy

Apollo sources Asset-Backed Finance investments across diversified channels:
- **Direct origination** through proprietary platforms
- **Partnerships** with originators and servicers
- **Primary market participation** in new issuance
- **Secondary market** opportunities at discount to par

The firm describes its approach as enabling "risk-adjusted return, accounting for structure, asset risk and yield."

## Competitive Positioning

Apollo's ABF platform advantages:
1. **Price Certainty** — ability to provide committed financing at scale
2. **Lender Alignment** — co-investment structures aligning incentives
3. **Relationship Simplicity** — single counterparty for complex structured solutions
4. **Scale** — global relationships and proprietary origination capabilities

## Why ABF Now (2024 Context)

Post-GFC bank regulatory capital requirements (Basel III/IV) have created a structural withdrawal of bank balance sheet from:
- Non-agency RMBS warehousing
- Structured credit origination
- Whole loan financing

This bank retrenchment creates a durable opportunity for large-scale alternative asset managers like Apollo to fill the gap as the "private replacement" for bank lending in structured finance.
